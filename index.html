About Me

I grew up in Cincinnati, Ohio. When I was 18 the military called to me and I served as an infantryman for four years in the United States Marine Corps. I wanted to challenge myself, and develop myself as much as possible, so I chose a path that I knew would be difficult for me. There, I learned invaluable lessons about leadership, accountability, and competitiveness. The Marine Corps is structured to develop leaders quickly, so I learned as much as I could and eventually got to lead my own team of four Marines. I got to travel the world and learn to work together with different cultures. At the end of my service in the Marines, I knew I wanted to apply my skills to the civilian world.

In Miami, I learned that I wanted to be apart of the fast moving world of software development. I knew that using code, I could possibly reach millions of people with my knowledge, and that inspires me.

Now, I am a junior attending Florida International University. I am really enjoying my coursework and love to learn industry related skills like web development in my free time. I like practicing OOP, as it is satisfying to understand the fundamentals of software engineering that I know will always be useful. Currently, I am employed as a research assistant where I help design surveys on crowdsourcing websites. We are researching virtual social agents for alcoholics. I find it easy to be passionate about a field that can help others.

In my free time, I enjoy spending time outdoors, hiking and camping. I also have a strong passion for music, and listen in my spare time.
